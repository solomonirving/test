About Me

To me, working on your car is personal. I feel it’s important that I tell a little bit about myself and why I am so passionate about what I do. My Name is Tyler. I am a husband to a wonderful woman who has always been my number one supporter. I have three beautiful children, who push me to do the best that I can ever do. I am originally from southern California. I moved to Sacramento when I was about five years old. I grew up turning wrenches with my father and hoped to do that with my kids.

I have a warm memory of my father working on a 1993 Honda Civic 1.5-liter engine that had a blown head gasket. I remember this specific time because that is when I learned the name of the tools he used and what the purpose was for each one. I was only six. At that very young age I learned organization, pride, and hard work. I find these three things to be very strong values that I follow with every project that I do. Organization is important because to my father, every part and tool had to have a place. Losing a part or misplacing a tool meant wasted time and money especially if you had to go out and replace it. Being disorganized also meant the possibility of damaging a part, this could lead to unnecessary spending.

Pride is of importance because the work you do represents who you are. To my father working on the car wasn’t a “do it yourself” task. This was a task that he and I did together. We bonded while trying to find the problem and learning how to fix it. Doing this work correctly was important because there was the possibility of selling the car one day or more importantly, handing it down to the ones we love. This also was tied to possibility that someone down the line would want to adjust the vehicle and being prideful of doing our job well would make it easier for them. Our hard work would then show the next person that we truly cared.

With hard work came the ability to adapt and learn. YouTube was not around when I was learning about cars. I studied Haynes manuals and dealer manuals in order to find the proper way of doing things. My favorite place to go with my father is a local store called Tognottis Auto World.

I always had a dream of starting my own business that would be an asset to my community. I went to school and studied business. I also loved the automotive industry and knew that I had a gift with cars. My passion for cars, guided me to UTI where I trained under the Toyota program. In the end, the two came together thus opening the doors for me to service you and your family’s automotive needs.
